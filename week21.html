Key Differences Between Freehold and Leasehold

Ownership Rights

Freehold properties offer full ownership, while leasehold properties come with a fixed-term, typically 30–99 years.

Investment Duration

Freehold properties are long-term investments, providing indefinite ownership, whereas leasehold investments have a defined duration before reversion to the landowner.

Property Control

Freehold owners have complete control over the property, while leasehold owners may face restrictions after the lease term expires.
